What we learned from Apollo, 60 years later

By Ada Horne · Feb 2026

The Apollo program was a series of crewed spaceflight missions undertaken by NASA between 1961 and 1972. It successfully landed the first humans on the Moon from 1969 to 1972. The program built on the Mercury and Gemini programs that preceded it.

Apollo 11 marked the first lunar landing in July 1969, with Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin walking on the lunar surface while Michael Collins orbited above. The crew returned with 21.5 kilograms of lunar material, and six subsequent missions returned larger hauls over the next three years.

Six of the seven later missions landed successfully on the Moon; the exception was Apollo 13, whose crew aborted the landing after an oxygen tank rupture and returned safely using the lunar module as a lifeboat.
