Cohen and Maher's paper on causal inference in distributed systems argues that standard randomized trials break down when subjects can coordinate out-of-band. They propose a family of estimators that remain unbiased under lateral communication, at the cost of wider confidence intervals.

  • Classical RCT assumptions require independent treatment assignment.
  • Lateral communication breaks independence even when assignment is random.
  • Empirical simulations show a 2.4× variance penalty in exchange for de-biasing.
